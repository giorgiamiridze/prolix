Is Kindle better than physical books?

  • By Giorgi Amiridze
  • February 5, 2023
  • No Comments

As the Kindle is rightly gaining popularity among people of different ages and sexes, an adequate question arises, is a Kindle Better Than Physical Books?" I'll try to briefly answer the question by saying yes! And to understand my rationale, I invite you to carefully consider the following nuances and details that led me to this conclusion after years of research and experimentation.

We suck at seeing immobile things.

Humans, just like frogs, suck at seeing immobile things by their evolutionary nature. If something doesn't move it remains a great challenge for us to see. The only reason why we somewhat see immobile things is because of our heartbeat, which gives our head and thus eyes a dynamic movement. When we link this trivial biological fact with reading, we understand that it's difficult for humans to read and comprehend when a book is on the table, unmoved, while reading. Although we can hold books in our hands while reading and thus enhance our comprehension, not all books are comfortable enough to be held in our hands. For this reason, Kindle is more beneficial for the reading process, since almost everyone reads a Kindle while holding it.

Font size, margins, and transparency of the paper, they all matter!

I don't think it's controversial to say that all the small nuances such as font family, font size, and margins matter. I've personally given up on reading a book because of its font size and bought a different copy of it with a convenient font size. Not only the font size but the transparency of the paper plays a significant role. I can go on and point out all the details that are important for our brains to have a productive reading, but I'm sure you get the idea. Kindle, on the other hand, gives us the opportunity to adjust everything to our personal convenience and thus necessarily enhance our comprehension and the overall process of reading.
